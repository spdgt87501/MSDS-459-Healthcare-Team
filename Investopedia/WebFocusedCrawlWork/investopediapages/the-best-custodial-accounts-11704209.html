A custodial account is a unique banking or investment account that authorizes an adult to control the financial assets in the account on behalf of a minor who is the account beneficiary. Immediate and extended family members, as well as legal guardians, can open a custodial account for a child under the age of majority, which ranges from 18 to 25, depending on the state. Many online brokerage firms offer custodial accounts to the general public. However, the best custodial accounts provide their users with a lot of services and account amenities while being cost-effective.

To find the best custodial accounts, Investopedia researched and analyzed nine leading online brokerage firms and compared each company against 34 criteria. We then selected each company based on our criteria and matched them to their most prominent strengths. Below is our list of the best custodial accounts.

Best Custodial Accounts for May 2025

  • Best Overall: Charles Schwab
  • Best for Low Fees: Fidelity Investments
  • Best Robo-Advisor: Acorns
  • Best for Mutual Funds: Vanguard
  • Best Custodial Bank Account: Ally

There are two types of custodial accounts to choose from, and while both accounts are designed for children under the age of majority, each account has different objectives.

The Uniform Gifts to Minors Act (UGMA) is an account where adults—often parents, grandparents, and guardians—manage financial assets such as cash and stocks until their transfer to a beneficiary when they reach their state’s age of adulthood. Meanwhile, the custodian can invest those assets on behalf of the beneficiary.

The Uniform Transfers to Minors Act (UTMA) is for more complex asset transfers. While a UTMA account can accept cash and stocks, it can also hold physical assets such as real estate and art, as well as patents and royalties. All states have adopted the UGMA in some form, but South Carolina and Vermont do not permit the use of a UTMA. To better understand what type of custodial account best suits you and the account beneficiary’s needs, speak with a financial advisor.

Best Overall : Charles Schwab

  • Account Minimum: $0
  • Fees or Commissions: $0 for stock/ETF trades, $0.65 per contract for options
  • Available Account Types Available: UGMA and UTMA (in states that allow them)
Open Account
Why We Chose It

We chose Charles Schwab as the best overall custodial account provider due to its cost-effective, transparent, and easy account setup process. It provides parents, guardians, and other extended family members with an easy and convenient way to fund the custodial account, which can be invested in stocks, mutual funds, and ETFs. In addition, Schwab provides excellent education tailored to children in various age groups.

Pros & Cons
Pros

  • Excellent selection of stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds, including derivatives

  • No fees, commissions, or minimum deposit to open an account

  • Excellent education and research amenities with 24/7 customer support

Cons

  • Does not offer fractional ETFs

  • Low interest rate for uninvested cash

  • High fees for miscellaneous services

Overview  

Charles Schwab was founded in 1971 and is headquartered in Westlake, Texas. It is one of the premier online brokerage firms and is our choice as the provider of the best overall custodial account for parents and guardians to consider. In addition to providing users with an easy account opening process, users can do so without having to shell out a minimum deposit.

Once the account is established in the child's name and adequately funded, the parent or guardian has many options for investing on behalf of the child, such as stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds. Parents who are skilled traders can also trade derivatives such as options and futures.

In addition to its rich trading features, Schwab also offers excellent education and research amenities. Children, parents, and guardians  can learn together about the importance of investing through Schwab's free educational resources. Users also have access to great research materials from outlets such as Morningstar, Argus, and CFRA alongside proprietary research provided by Schwab itself.

While Schwab won for the best overall custodial account, prospective users should consider its drawbacks. For one, Schwab does not offer users the opportunity to invest in fractional ETFs; however, it does allow trading in fractional stocks. Users also earn a paltry interest rate of just 0.05% on uninvested cash. Schwab also charges high fees for some of its services, such as account transfers of $50, wire fees of $25, and broker-assisted trades of $25. Still, customers who need help can contact customer service 24 hours, seven days a week.

Best for Low Fees : Fidelity Investments

  • Account Minimum: $0 
  • Fees or Commissions: $0 for stock/ETF trades, $0 plus $0.65/contract for options trades
  • Available Account Types: UGMA and UTMA (in states that allow them)
Open Account
Why We Chose It

We chose Fidelity Investments as the provider of the best custodial account for low fees due to its $0 fee schedule for financial instruments such as stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and ETFs. In addition, many of the account services that Fidelity Investments offers have $0 fees.

Pros & Cons
Pros

  • An excellent low-cost custodial account option

  • A 3.99% interest rate for uninvested cash

  • Offers fractional shares of stocks and ETFs

Cons

  • Its robo-advisor does not support custodial accounts

  • High transaction fees for non-Fidelity mutual funds

  • High service fees for certain ETFs

Overview

Fidelity Investments was founded in 1946 and has its headquarters in Boston, Massachusetts. It is our choice for users seeking a custodial account provider that values low fees but offers high-quality service, including robust trading features, research amenities, and the ability to invest in fractional shares of stocks and ETFs.

Parents and guardians who want to open a custodial account with Fidelity Investments can choose between a UGMA or a UTMA account without a minimum deposit. In addition, they can enjoy $0 fees or commissions for stocks, bonds, ETFs, and Fidelity-operated mutual funds. Fidelity also allows trading in options in custodial accounts, which are subject to a $0.65 fee per contract.

The custodial account also comes equipped with an excellent array of research tools and resources. For instance, custodians have access to multiple screeners for stocks and mutual funds. Fidelity also provides proprietary research along with access to external professional research sources, the most notable being CFRA, Morningstar, Argus, and Zacks Investment Research.

However, it is important to note the drawbacks of Fidelity Investments. Fidelity Go, the robo-advisor for the online brokerage firm, does not allow users to open a custodial account. While Fidelity is the best provider of custodial accounts with low fees, it does asses some hefty fees that prospective clients should be aware of. Non-Fidelity mutual funds come with a $49.95 transaction fee, but Fidelity offers a good selection of no-transaction fees mutual funds as an alternative. Still, Fidelity does charge upwards of $100 for some ETFs in its custodial accounts. However, Fidelity offers many ETFs that are not subject to fees.

Best Robo-Advisor : Acorns

  • Account Minimum: $0 
  • Fees or Commissions: Bronze: $3/month, Silver: $6/month, Gold: $12/month
  • Available Account Types: UGMA and UTMA (in states that allow them)
Open Account
Why We Chose It

We chose Acorns as the best robo-advisor provider of custodial accounts due to the unique and valuable features its robo-advisor offers to help build wealth for the benefit of the child.

Pros & Cons
Pros

  • Excellent resources to learn about investing

  • Matching contributions between 1% to 3% for an Acorns Later IRA

  • Spare change round-up feature

Cons

  • Monthly subscription fees

  • Only stocks and ETF investment selections

  • Does not have research tools or resources

Overview  

Acorns was founded in 2012 and is headquartered in Irvine, California. Due to its rich features and beginner-friendly approach, it is our choice for parents and guardians seeking to automate their child’s custodial accounts.

Acorns custodial accounts can be opened with a $0 minimum deposit. While stocks and ETFs don’t have a fee associated with them, Acorns itself levies monthly tiered-level subscription fees. For example, Acorns Bronze costs $3 monthly, Acorns Silver costs $6 monthly, and Acorns Gold costs $12 monthly. Not surprisingly, the higher the subscription, the better the features, amenities, and perks a user is entitled to.  

Even though Acorns charges a subscription fee, it has a lot to offer that many other robo-advisors do not. For instance, custodians have access to checking and retirement services. Furthermore, depending on your subscription, users can get a 1% or 3% percent match on contributions to their children's IRA. Another valuable feature is the spare change service Acorns offers. Custodians can link a credit or debit card to their Acorns account and deposit the spare change into that account on expenditures outside of Acorns by rounding up to the nearest dollar.   

Another plus is that Acorns offers educational opportunities in the form of lessons, quizzes, videos, stories, and so on to help children of all ages learn about topics such as investing, retirement, borrowing, and planning. However, Acorns does not offer research tools or proprietary financial reports from services such as Morningstar and Argus. Custodians should also understand that the robo-advisor's investment selection is limited to stocks and ETFs.

Best for Mutual Funds : Vanguard

  • Account Minimum: $0  
  • Fees or Commissions: $0 for stocks and ETFs
  • Available Account Types: UGMA and UTMA (in states that allow them)
Open Account
Why We Chose It

We chose Vanguard as the best online brokerage firm to offer mutual funds for custodial accounts. Custodians can choose from over 160 Vanguard-operated mutual funds and over 3,000 non-Vanguard funds on behalf of the beneficiary.

Pros & Cons
Pros

  • An excellent selection of mutual funds

  • A 3.65% APY interest rate for uninvested cash

  • Good research tools and amenities

Cons

  • $25 annual fee to open a custodial account

  • Does not offer fractional stocks and non-Vanguard ETFs

  • Up to $3,000 minimum investment for most Vanguard mutual funds

Overview

Founded in 1975 and headquartered in Valley Forge, Pennsylvania, Vanguard is our choice for the best provider of mutual funds for custodial accounts due to its rich selection, which includes over 160 Vanguard-operated mutual funds and 3,000 non-Vanguard mutual funds. Vanguard also allows its users to invest in stocks and ETFs. Keep in mind that Vanguard does not offer fractional shares of stocks and non-Vanguard ETFs. For now, it only offers fractional shares for Vanguard-operated ETFs.

If you open an account, you get excellent service. For instance, users get a big lineup of good research tools, including a screener that can help identify stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds that meet your specific criteria, as well as calculators that can be useful to help plan for retirement, college, and much more. As for professional research analysis, Vanguard provides its own proprietary reports along with reports from institutions such as Argus and Market Grader. Vanguard also provides an excellent interest rate for uninvested cash at 3.65% APY.

However, don’t ignore potential drawbacks before opening a custodial account. Every account opened at Vanguard is subject to a $25 annual fee. However, the fee is fairly easy to avoid by signing up for the e-delivery of statements and other important notices that Vanguard is required to send. Potentially more daunting for some account openers is the $3,000 minimum initial investment required for most Vanguard mutual funds, which is steep for a custodial account.

Best Custodial Bank Account : Ally

  • Account Minimum: $0  
  • Fees or Commissions: N/A 
  • Available Account Types: UGMA and UTMA (in states that allow them)
Open Account
Why We Chose It

We chose Ally as the provider of the best custodial bank account due to its versatile banking options. Those who take a more conservative approach to building wealth in a custodial account may want to consider a banking platform such as Ally Bank.

Pros & Cons
Pros

  • Multiple FDIC-insured banking options to choose

  • A 3.60% annual percentage yield (APY) for savings and Money Market accounts

  • Easy account opening process with $0 account minimum

Cons

  • Brokerage services are not available for custodial accounts

  • Does not have physical locations

  • 0.10% interest rate for its checking account

Overview

Ally was founded in 1919 and is headquartered in Detroit, Michigan. It is our choice for the provider of the best custodial bank account. While many brokerage firms offer custodial accounts with the added ability to invest in stocks and ETFs, Ally Bank is designed for risk-averse users who only need banking features to build their custodial accounts.

Users can open FDIC-insured checking, savings, or money market accounts, as well as certificates of deposit (CD) for their beneficiaries. Those types of accounts have a lower risk of volatility and loss of principal than accounts that let you invest in securities that are subject to market fluctuations. Since Ally Bank's custodial account is operated by banking features, it’s important to note the differences in interest rates for each account.

For instance, Ally’s custodial checking account has a 0.10% APY, whereas its savings and money market custodial accounts currently enjoy an interest rate of 3.60% APY. With its CD offering, users can choose between three different types of CDs, which include a high-yield CD, a “Raise Your Rate” CD, and a no penalty CD.

Rates are subject to change, so check them before opening an account. Users should also be aware that Ally is an online bank without brick-and-mortar locations. Ally’s custodial accounts are built with bank features and cannot offer stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds. If a bank is what you are looking for to drive your custodial account, parents and guardians can easily open an account without an account minimum.
